Financial Glossary

Understand financial terms and concepts used throughout the calculators

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)

Loans

A fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is fully paid off along with interest.

Principal Amount

General

The original sum of money borrowed in a loan or invested in an investment vehicle. This is the base amount on which interest is calculated.

Interest Rate

General

The percentage of principal charged by the lender for the use of its money. For investments, it represents the rate of return on the investment.

Extra Payment

Loans

Additional payment made towards a loan principal beyond the required EMI. This helps reduce the overall interest paid and loan tenure.

Loan Tenure

Loans

The total duration or term of the loan, typically expressed in months or years. It determines how long you have to repay the loan.

Amortization Schedule

Loans

A complete table of periodic loan payments showing the amount of principal and interest that comprise each payment until the loan is paid off at the end of its term.

Remaining Balance

Loans

The amount of principal that remains to be paid on a loan at any given time.

Loan Cost Ratio

Loans

The ratio of total interest paid to the original loan amount, expressed as a percentage. It helps evaluate the true cost of borrowing.

Effective Interest Rate

Loans

The actual cost of borrowing when considering compounding and all associated fees, often different from the stated interest rate.

Compound Interest

Investments

Interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. This makes your money grow faster compared to simple interest.

Initial Investment

Investments

The starting amount you invest, also known as the principal investment. This is the base amount that begins earning returns.

Monthly Investment

Investments

Regular contributions made to your investment portfolio each month, helping to build wealth through consistent investing.

Expected Annual Return

Investments

The percentage gain or loss on an investment over a one-year period that you anticipate earning.

Portfolio Value

Investments

The total market value of all investments held in an investment portfolio at a given time.

Investment Time Horizon

Investments

The length of time you expect to hold an investment before needing the money, affecting your investment strategy and risk tolerance.

Passive Income

Investments

Income earned from investments that require minimal ongoing effort to maintain, such as dividend income or interest payments.

Total Investment Value

Investments

The current worth of your investment, including both your contributions and any returns earned.

Wealth Accumulation Rate

Investments

The speed at which your investment portfolio grows, calculated as the percentage of earnings relative to total contributions.

Break-even Point

Advanced Analysis

The point at which total investment returns equal the total loan costs, helping compare investment versus loan prepayment strategies.

Real Return

Advanced Analysis

The return on investment after accounting for inflation, representing actual purchasing power gains.

Investment vs Loan Ratio

Advanced Analysis

A comparison metric showing the relative performance of investing versus loan prepayment strategies.

Safe Withdrawal Rate

Advanced Analysis

The percentage of savings you can withdraw annually without depleting your portfolio, typically 3-4% for retirement planning.

Financial Independence

Advanced Analysis

The state of having sufficient personal wealth to live without having to work actively for basic necessities.

Inflation Rate

Economics

The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, consequently decreasing purchasing power over time.

Tax Bracket

Taxation

A range of incomes taxed at a specific rate. Higher income typically falls into higher tax brackets, affecting the after-tax returns on investments.

Tax Implications

Taxation

The effect of taxes on investment returns or loan interest deductions, important for calculating actual gains or savings.

Tax Deduction

Taxation

An expense you can subtract from your taxable income, such as home loan interest, reducing your overall tax liability.

Fixed Deposit (FD)

Investment Products

A financial instrument provided by banks which provides investors a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account, until the given maturity date.

Public Provident Fund (PPF)

Investment Products

A long-term investment scheme backed by the Government of India which offers tax benefits and guaranteed returns.

National Pension System (NPS)

Investment Products

A government-sponsored pension scheme that encourages people to invest in a pension account at regular intervals during their employment.

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

Investment Products

A method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds, similar to recurring deposits in banks.
